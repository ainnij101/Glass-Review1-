    7.6.3 VISUAL ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

    The glass shall be clean cut, without significant edge faults (including feathered edges, shells or other imperfections) and free from bubbles, inclusions, cracks ripping dimples or other defects. Distortion shall be kept to an absolute minimum and no local defects producing irregular reflections shall be allowed.

    The quality of all glass shall be assessed for optical and visual faults as described in BS EN 572-2 and the requirement specified in section 7.6.1, 7.6.6 and 7.6.7 of this Specification. Spot faults shall not be worse than category C. There shall be no linear/extended faults. For optical faults,acceptance levels shall be as described in the standard.

    In the absence of a British standard covering visual process blemishes in laminated glass, reference shall be made to ASTM C 1172 Table 3 for architectural quality glass.

    Unless otherwise accepted by the Contract Administrator, three (3) 1x1m glass samples of each type, with the same tinting, heat treatment, coating and build-up, shall be submitted, prior to commencement on site, showing the final quality of the finished glass. The sample shall be to a standard agreed by the Contract Administrator.

    The accepted trial samples shall be kept by the Contract Administrator as control samples (benchmark) of the acceptable range of visual deviations. Also refer to section 7.6.1, 7.6.6, 7.6.7 and 9.4.2, for acceptance criterion on glass visual quality.

    7.6.1 GENERAL

    Glass shall comply with the standards as specified in section 6 and the description in section 5.2.8 of this Specification.

    Submit samples of glass in accordance with SCHEDULE 2.

    The thickness and dimensions of glass shown on the Drawings are not prescribed thickness or dimensions. The Contractor shall calculate the required glass thickness using the minimum thickness principle to meet the structural, thermal, environmental, acoustic and safety requirements. Test report shall be prepared by the recognised independent test laboratory shall be submitted and reviewed without objection by the Contract Administrator prior to material ordering.

    One hundred percent heat soaking shall be required for all glass exceeding surface and edge stresses of 7500psi (52 N/mm²). Contractor shall submit heat soak process and QC program for review without objection by Contract Administrator to ensure conformance with requirements listed in EN 14179 with calibrated heat soak ovens. Temperature-time curve for heat soak testing of each batch of processed glass shall be recorded real-time and be witnessed by representative of the Contractor. All heat soak test records issued by the glass manufacturer and countersigned by the contractor shall be submitted to the Contract Administrator for review following delivery of glass to the assembly factory or to site. Heat-treated glass such as Heat strengthened glass and tempered glass shall be examined by the glass manufacturer to detect and discard any lites which exceed the following tolerances:

    1) Glass that has any deviation from flat (bow) of 0.10% of the shortest glass dimension.

    2) Where the heat treating process results in essentially parallel ripples or waves (roller waves), the deviation from flatness at any peak shall not exceed 0.13mm, and the difference between adjacent peaks shall not exceed 0.08mm.

    3) Where bow tolerance and roller wave tolerance differ, the stricter requirements shall govern.

    4) Direction of ripples shall be horizontal, consistent, and in conformance with architectural design. The appearance of anisotropy or "Leopard Spots", a multi-coloured pattern, is known to be associated with heat treated glass under certain polarised lighting conditions. This shall not be considered a fault unless it is visible in a range of reasonably typical naturally occurring conditions. Any coatings applied to the glass must not increase the tendency to show these effects and samples must be submitted to the Contractor Administrator for review without objection. Control samples, as indicated in SCHEDULE 2, shall be submitted for review without objection prior to ordering of the HKPM project glass.

    5) Bubbles and inclusions in glass panels are considered as unacceptable.

    7.6.6 TEMPERED GLASS

    The Contractor must satisfy himself that glass tempering requirements are satisfactory to meet wind or other loads anticipated in the works. The prime manufacturer of the tempered glass shall be made aware of its intended use in the construction. Any drilling and notching shall be done with the agreement of the prime manufacturer of toughened glass and prior to the toughening being carried out. All toughened glass shall be tempered on a roller hearth furnace eliminating tong marks and shall conform to BS 6206 Class A.

    All tempered glass shall be off line (i.e. glass allowed to cool down to room temperature for 24 hours prior to heat soaking) heat soak tested to EN 14179 by the glass manufacturer’s calibrated heat soak ovens equipped with digital thermal couples connected to a computer data acquisition system. Real time temperature-time curve for heat soaking of each batch of processed glass shall be recorded and be witnessed by the independent laboratory appointed by the Contractor. The accredited laboratory for the oven calibration and witnessing the heat soak process shall be nominated during tender stage for the Contract Administrator’s review without objection.

    All heat soak test records issued by the glass manufacturer shall be countersigned by the accredited independent laboratory and the Contractor and shall be submitted to the Contract Administrator for review following glass delivery to the assembly factory or to site. After the test the probability of failure in service shall be less than one in 400 tonnes. The Contractor shall ensure that the manufacturer shall, at the request of the Contract Administrator, show by statistical analysis of test data that the probability of failure is not greater than the value quoted above.

    Edge working shall be flat ground with small ground arris and have a frosted appearance. Corners shall be dubbed. Small shells and/or chips shall be ground out prior to toughening. Maximum chip/shell diameter shall not exceed 2mm, and there shall not be more than four randomly placed chip/shells in any single pane of glass.

    The surface compressive stress shall be demonstrated by non-destructive testing to be controlled at the factory within the range of 70 N/mm² to 100 N/mm².

    Unless otherwise accepted by the Contract Administrator, three (3) 1x1m samples of each with designated tinting, coating, heat treatment and built-up for each thickness of glass to demonstrate the roller wave, leopard spots, strain patterns, colour variations (tinting and coating), edge deletion, sealant quality and the like that shall be obtained. The accepted samples shall be kept by the Contract Administrator as control samples (benchmark) of the acceptable range of visual deviations.



    7.6.7 HEAT STRENGTHENED GLASS

    All heat strengthened glass shall be tempered on a roller hearth furnace eliminating tong marks.

    Edge working shall be flat ground with ground arris and have a frosted appearance. Corners shall be dubbed.

    Small shells and/or chips shall be ground out prior to toughening. Maximum chip/shell diameter shall not exceed 2mm, and there shall not be more than four randomly placed chip/shells in any single pane of glass.

    The surface compressive stress shall be demonstrated by non-destructive testing to be between 24 N/mm² and 52 N/mm².

    Unless otherwise accepted by the Contract Administrator, the Contractor shall submit three (3) 1x1m samples of glass each with designated tinting, coating, heat treatment and built-up for each thickness of glass to demonstrate the roller wave, leopard spots, strain patterns, colour variations (tinting and coating) and the like that will be obtained.

    The accepted samples shall be kept by the Contract Administrator as control samples (benchmark) of the acceptable range of visual deviations.



    7.6.8 LAMINATED GLASS

    Laminated glass shall conform to ANSI Z97.1, BS 952, BS 6206, BS 6262 and ASTM C 1172.

    Laminated glass units shall be fabricated with autoclaved edges on all four sides. All edges and cuts shall be accurate, clean, sharp, square, smooth and free of burrs. For all point fixing glazing, the glass edges shall be arrised with full surface grinding.

    For all framed glazing, the glass edges shall be arrised and the chamfer shall be ground.

    There shall be no delamination or shrinkage excessively at the edges of laminated glass or panels.

    The Contractor shall replace all delaminated glass panels within the warranty; all associated costs, including but not limited to scaffolding, material and labour shall be borne solely by the Contractor.

    The minimum thickness of the interlayer shall be 1.52mm. All laminated glass edges (specifically the PVB interlayer) shall be protected from contact with silicone sealants or exposure to the atmosphere.

    All glass holes shall be chamfered and ground. SGP interlayer shall be used where indicated in clause 5.2.8.



    7.6.9 LOW EMISSIVITY COATING

    Low emissivity coating (Low-E), if required to achieve the performance criteria specified, shall be nominally neutral in colour and uniform in tone, hue, colour, texture, pattern and opacity and shall provide a consistent appearance to the glazed units.

    Edge deletion shall be required. Where coatings are deleted at edges, no visible edge of stripped glass shall show, and the coating and butyl seal shall not overlap by more than 2mm.

    Refer to section 7.6.3 on control samples of coated glass.



    7.6.10 LOW IRON GLASS

    Low iron glass shall conform to the following minimum requirement and be subject to the Contract Administrator’s review without objection for clarity:

    1) 100 parts per million either in the form of Fe2O3 or FeO2 or both.

    2) Control sample provided by Consultant team



    7.6.11 FIRE RESISTANT GLAZING

    Proprietary fire resistant glass shall be provided where shown on the Drawings (minimum 1-hour fire resistance rating).

    Glass including multi-layer intumescent laminated glass shall be successfully tested in accordance with BS 476: Part 22.1987 and BS 6206:1981, Class A requirement.

    All tests shall include the glass and the framing system and shall be conducted by HOKLAS approved independent test laboratory.

    For any oversize fire rated glazing panels, the Contractor shall conduct fire testing as required for the glass and framing system. Fire test shall be conducted by HOKLAS approved independent test laboratory.



    7.6.12 ANTI-SLIP GLAZING

    Where shown on the Drawings, anti-slip pattern shall be provided to top surface of external walk-on glazing.

    Carborundum frit anti-slip pattern shall be provided at surface #1 (pattern as shown on the Drawing).

    Contractor shall carry our all necessary testing to demonstrate the glass surface is able to achieve slip-resistance with a static coefficient of friction as below:

    1) Dry condition: above 0.75 of static coefficient of friction in accordance with Appendix C of Barrier Free Access Design Manual 2008

    2) Wet condition: above 0.65



    7.6.13 CERAMIC FRIT

    Ceramic Frit, with the specified pattern, colour and the percentage of the opacity specified by the Contract Administrator shall be required.

    Edge deletion shall be required. Where frits are deleted at edges, no visible edge of stripped glass shall show.

    Frit shall not be in contact with either butyl seal or structural sealant.

    The max. overlap shall be controlled within 2mm.

    Refer to the Drawings and 5.2.8 Glass table for the design extent.

    As specified in SUOG 2018 part 4.4.5, bending tests are required in accordance with BS EN 1288-3.



    9.4.1 GENERAL

    Glazing shall be carried out in accordance with manufacturer's recommendations, which shall consider the recommendations of the Glass and Glazing Federation, the requirements of BS 6262, and other relevant standards listed in section 6.

    Setting blocks and anti-walk devices shall be installed in accordance with relevant standards.

    No grinding or welding shall be permitted within 10m of unprotected glass.



    9.4.2 VISUAL ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA – VIEWING AREAL

    When viewed from any angle or direction:

    1) Scratches and sleeks visible from 1000mm viewing distance shall be deemed unacceptable for glazed areas with long view only.

    2) Bubbles and inclusions greater than 1mm shall be deemed unacceptable for glazed areas with close viewing criteria.

    3) Bubbles less than 300mm apart shall be deemed unacceptable for glazed areas with close viewing criteria.

    4) Streaking, strain patterns, blotches, mottle shall not be visible when viewed from a distance of 3m perpendicular to the glass surface and when viewed from the range of positions within 60º measured from either side of this perpendicular viewing point.

    The Contract Administrator shall have the absolute right to request the Contractor to replace any glass units that in the Contract Administrator’s professional opinion do not satisfy this criteria; the Contractor shall have no claim whatsoever on cost and time.

    5) Installed glass shall comply with the requirements of the Guideline to Assessing the Visible Quality of Insulating Glass Unit, prepared by the Institute of the Glazing Trade for Glazing Technology and Window Manufacture in Hadamar, Germany.
